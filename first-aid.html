First Aid Guide

Immediate first aid can make a significant difference in an emergency. This guide provides step-by-step instructions for common medical situations. Always prioritize calling emergency services if the situation is severe.

Disclaimer: This First Aid Guide is for informational purposes only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. In a medical emergency, always call your local emergency services (e.g., 108 in India) immediately. If in doubt, seek professional medical help.

Common First Aid Scenarios

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)

For an adult who is unresponsive and not breathing normally:

  1. Call for help: Call 108 (or your local emergency number) immediately.
  2. Position the person: Lay the person on their back on a firm surface.
  3. Chest compressions:
    • Place the heel of one hand in the center of the person's chest, then place the other hand on top.
    • Keep your arms straight and position your shoulders directly over your hands.
    • Push hard, at least 2 inches (5 cm) deep, and fast, about 100 to 120 compressions per minute.
  4. Give rescue breaths (if trained):
    • After 30 compressions, gently tilt the head back and lift the chin to open the airway.
    • Pinch the nose shut, make a complete seal over their mouth, and give 2 rescue breaths (each lasting about 1 second).
    • Watch for chest rise with each breath.
  5. Continue: Keep cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until help arrives or the person shows signs of life.

Severe Bleeding

For serious cuts or wounds that are bleeding heavily:

  1. Apply direct pressure: Use a clean cloth, sterile bandage, or even your hand to apply firm, direct pressure to the wound.
  2. Elevate: If possible, raise the injured body part above the heart.
  3. Maintain pressure: Keep applying pressure until the bleeding stops or emergency medical help arrives. Do not remove the first layer of dressing if it becomes soaked; add more layers on top.
  4. Bandage: Once bleeding stops, secure the dressing with a bandage, but not so tightly that it cuts off circulation.
  5. Seek medical help: Even if bleeding stops, a severe wound needs professional medical attention.

Burns

For minor to moderate burns (first and second-degree):

  1. Cool the burn: Immediately hold the burned area under cool (not cold) running water for at least 10-20 minutes. Do not use ice.
  2. Remove jewelry/clothing: Gently remove any rings, watches, or clothing near the burned area before swelling occurs.
  3. Cover the burn: Loosely cover the burn with a sterile, non-stick bandage or clean cloth.
  4. Pain relief: Consider over-the-counter pain relievers if needed.
  5. Seek medical help: For large burns, deep burns, burns to the face, hands, feet, or genitals, or any burn causing extreme pain, seek immediate medical attention.

Fractures/Sprains

For suspected bone fractures or severe sprains:

  1. Immobilize: Do not try to straighten the injured limb. Immobilize the injured area using a splint (e.g., rigid material wrapped with bandages) or by supporting it with soft padding.
  2. Apply ice: Apply an ice pack wrapped in a cloth to the injured area for 15-20 minutes at a time to reduce swelling and pain.
  3. Elevate: Elevate the injured limb above the heart if possible.
  4. Seek medical help: Transport the person to a hospital or clinic for diagnosis and treatment.
